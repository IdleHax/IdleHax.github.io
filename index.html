Programming.

Hours of googling and a drive bordering on self-destructive to make a computer do things no one else intended, or wanted it to can produce something pretty special. I put together idie games from time to time and might post up how I solved the problems i ran into for others to learn from or laugh at.

Hacking.

I specialize in breaking things. My own things. Cracking hashes, breaking webpages, mangling software, voiding hardware, "ricing" operating systems and playing CTF's. I'll be doing write ups on all the legal ways you can too. Setting up a local server to host vulnerable webpages is a blast and a great way to crack your hacking knuckles without winding up in a black van.

Making.

I think myself a jack-of-all-trades-master-of-none in this catagory. I frequently design things in OpenSCAD to make my life easier. I've heavily modified my 3D printer and get some great prints on the cheap. Soldering is a fun and useful skill I'll also be covering. and who doesn't love turning early 2000's mp3 players into half-dollar sized gameboys?

Reversing.

Ahh yes. Assembly language, binary, and all of the lovely things your uncle had to slog through to make a "fortune telling" homebrew game that just insulted his mates regardless of what number they put in during the 80's. On a serious note, reverse engineering malware has been a critical tool for security experts the world around, and makes a good way to kill a weekend or render a virtual machine completely fragged in the process.
